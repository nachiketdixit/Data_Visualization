Part II - Effects of User types and Gender over Trips¶

by Nachiket Dixit¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of user types and gender that could be used to predict their day to day trip duration. The main focus was on the duration of trips.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consists of 1,83,412 records in the dataset with 16 features. The attributes included in the interested featues in the dataset such as duration, customer, subscriber, male, female and age. Only 1 member having age 143, and couple around age 120 were observed, removing these outliers did not cause any significant deviation.

Distribution of Trip Duration¶

Trip duration in the dataset take on a very large of values, from about 50 sec at the lowest, to about 5K sec at the highest. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of trip duration takes on a unimodal shape.

Distribution of Trip Count¶

The number of subscribers are more than customer and males are prefering majority trips as compared with females. Most of the users irrespective of type and gender are preferring not to share the bike during the trip.

Interestingly, customers are taking long duration trips than subscriber. Same pattern is with gender, females are taking long duration trips than males. If we look at the bike sharing during trip, people are reluctant to share bike during long duration trip as well.

Trip Duration by User type on Day of Week¶

It appears that the maximum trip duration are for weekend trips taken by both Customer and Subscriber. There is considerable amount of difference in trip duration between weekend trips and weekday trips taken by Customer. The trip duration difference for Subscriber between weekend and weekdays is not that much.

Trip Duration by Gender on Day of Week¶

Female customers are taking long duration trips as compare to male customer and the difference is considerable. If we look at the subscriber, here also females are taking more long duration trips but the trip duration difference between them is comparatively less.
